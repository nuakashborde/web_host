Debug Tool for Service Centers

PCB Design Project
Summer 2020

If a car needs servicing because of infotainment failures, this tool is used at service centers use to diagnose and repair the error. The debug adapter is a standalone circuit board that can be plugged into the USB port of a technician’s laptop, granting access directly to the car’s internal computer. With this circuitry, an engineer or mechanic can evaluate the vehicle’s log files, reset faulty programs, or flash new firmware directly into the car. In addition to simplifying the vehicle debugging process, this product reduced costs of each car by (~$10) removing non-essential debug components from the production line.

I oversaw the creation of the adapter from concept to reality as an engineer and project manager. I built a PCB for signal transmission and transformation between the laptop and car computer, created an enclosure for the board, and coordinated the board’s manufacturing and testing for production and deployment at Tesla’s 300+ service centers around the world.
