NUsolar

NUsolar is a student vehicle design team at Northwestern University (link) which designs, manufactures, and races solar-powered electric vehicles. Every 2-4 years, NUsolar completes the full build cycle of a solar vehicle, giving members opportunities in all aspects of the team. I was a four-year member of NUsolar, overlapping with the build cycles of cars SC6 (italics) and SC7 (italics), and served many roles on the team: electrical lead, operations manager, logistics lead, and general member.

Array Project

The solar array is a distinguishing feature of all solar cars. Its role is to convert the sun's electromagnetic radiation into electrical energy to charge the car's batteries. I was the lead engineer on the solar array design project, and completed the following tasks:

  • Solar Cell Selection
  • Circuit Design
  • Arrangment, Encapsulation, and Mounting

Arrangment

The cell arrangements had to be designed to fit on the car's top shell.

The layout of the cells also needed to be designed for easy wiring and robustness.

Cells would be encapsulated into "modules" which grouped several cells in one package for installation.

Encapsulation

Before installing on the car, the solar cells were protected in a process called encapuslation.

Solar cells are fragile and need to be protected from mechanical and thermal damage.

The cells are sandwiched in layers of polymers for protection.

To avoid damaging the cells, the encapsulation process requires a very controlled environment. This process was outsourced to a manufacturer, for which I was the main point of contact.
